# Executive Presentation Template

## Purpose

Use this outline to present GitHub to executive leadership, investment committees, or transformation sponsors. Each slide includes the message objective, suggested content, speaker notes, and talking points.

---

## Slide 1 - Title / Decision Frame

### Slide Title
**Standardizing on GitHub as the Enterprise Software Platform**

### On-Slide Content
- Current-state platform fragmentation
- Need for faster, safer software delivery
- Opportunity to consolidate around GitHub

### Speaker Notes
Set the tone: this is not a tooling demo; it is a strategic decision about how the organization builds software, applies AI, and reduces delivery friction.

### Talking Points
- Software delivery is now a strategic capability, not just an IT function.
- Our current platform landscape creates avoidable cost and complexity.
- GitHub offers a path to standardization, automation, and AI-enabled productivity.

---

## Slide 2 - Problem Statement

### Slide Title
**Why Change Now**

### On-Slide Content
| Current Challenge | Business Impact |
| --- | --- |
| Tool sprawl across SCM, CI/CD, security, and collaboration | Higher total cost and fragmented governance |
| Slow onboarding and inconsistent workflows | Lower developer productivity |
| Security controls too late in the lifecycle | Higher remediation cost and risk |
| Uncoordinated AI experiments | Policy gaps and missed productivity gains |

### Speaker Notes
Make the cost of inaction explicit. Leadership should feel the operational drag of the current state.

### Talking Points
- We are paying a tax for fragmentation every day.
- Platform inconsistency slows delivery and makes governance harder.
- AI makes this decision more urgent because platform choice now shapes engineering productivity.

---

## Slide 3 - Why GitHub

### Slide Title
**Why GitHub Is the Strategic Choice**

### On-Slide Content
- 180M+ developers; 90%+ of Fortune 100 rely on GitHub
- Microsoft-backed platform with enterprise-grade scale
- Unified capabilities across code, automation, security, and AI
- Strong developer preference and ecosystem reach

### Speaker Notes
Anchor on external validation and platform breadth before getting into economics.

### Talking Points
- GitHub combines market leadership with strong enterprise relevance.
- Developers already know it, which reduces adoption friction.
- It is one of the clearest ways to standardize modern software delivery.

---

## Slide 4 - Solution Overview

### Slide Title
**What We Standardize On**

### On-Slide Content
- GitHub Enterprise
- GitHub Actions
- GitHub Advanced Security
- GitHub Copilot
- Supporting governance, identity, and reusable workflows

### Speaker Notes
Keep this simple and outcome-oriented. The audience does not need feature depth.

### Talking Points
- This is a platform model, not a point product purchase.
- The core value comes from integration across these capabilities.
- Standardization improves both speed and control.

---

## Slide 5 - Business Value

### Slide Title
**Expected Business Value**

### On-Slide Content
| Value Driver | Example Impact |
| --- | --- |
| Developer productivity | Research and pilots often show 20-55% gains in targeted tasks |
| Faster CI/CD | Reduced build/deploy friction and more reusable automation |
| Security cost avoidance | Earlier detection lowers remediation and incident cost |
| Tool consolidation | Fewer vendors, less integration overhead |
| Talent retention | Better developer experience and employer brand |

### Speaker Notes
Use a conservative scenario first, then show upside.

### Talking Points
- Even modest productivity gains create outsized value at our scale.
- Security and CI/CD improvements add meaningful financial benefit.
- The combined value typically outweighs license cost by a wide margin.

---

## Slide 6 - TCO View

### Slide Title
**Total Cost of Ownership, Not Just License Cost**

### On-Slide Content
- License cost
- Platform administration
- CI/CD compute
- Integration and maintenance
- Training/change cost
- Retired-tool savings

### Speaker Notes
This is the CFO slide. Show that the real comparison is cost-to-operate the software platform.

### Talking Points
- Lowest seat price does not equal lowest total cost.
- Consolidation is where the economics become compelling.
- Platform complexity scales faster than license spend.

---

## Slide 7 - Competitive Analysis

### Slide Title
**Why GitHub Wins Against Alternatives**

### On-Slide Content
| Competitor | Strength | Why GitHub Still Leads |
| --- | --- | --- |
| GitLab | Integrated DevSecOps story | GitHub has stronger developer pull, ecosystem, and AI mindshare |
| Azure DevOps | Legacy Microsoft process fit | GitHub is better aligned to future-state collaboration and AI workflows |
| Bitbucket | Atlassian adjacency | GitHub has stronger community, talent attraction, and platform momentum |

### Speaker Notes
Be balanced. Credibility increases when trade-offs are acknowledged.

### Talking Points
- Alternatives can fit specific legacy contexts.
- GitHub is the stronger long-term platform for modernization and developer adoption.
- The decision should optimize future-state engineering performance, not preserve yesterday's process.

---

## Slide 8 - Implementation Timeline

### Slide Title
**Phased Rollout Plan**

### On-Slide Content
| Phase | Timeline | Objective |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1. Assess | 0-30 days | Baseline tools, teams, workflows, and costs |
| 2. Pilot | 30-90 days | Prove value with selected teams and workloads |
| 3. Expand | 90-180 days | Scale workflows, security, and enablement |
| 4. Standardize | 6-12 months | Consolidate tooling and embed operating model |

### Speaker Notes
Show that adoption is manageable and staged.

### Talking Points
- We do not need a big-bang migration.
- Value can be demonstrated early with a pilot.
- Governance and enablement should scale with rollout.

---

## Slide 9 - Investment Ask

### Slide Title
**What We Need Approved**

### On-Slide Content
- Platform licensing / enterprise agreement
- Optional add-ons such as Copilot and Advanced Security
- Enablement and migration support
- Executive sponsorship for platform standardization

### Speaker Notes
Be explicit about both budget and organizational sponsorship.

### Talking Points
- Budget alone is insufficient without leadership support for standardization.
- The investment includes change management, not just software.
- We should align procurement to the target operating model.

---

## Slide 10 - Risk Mitigation

### Slide Title
**How We De-Risk Adoption**

### On-Slide Content
- phased rollout
- targeted migration waves
- measurable success criteria
- security and compliance review upfront
- developer champions and training

### Speaker Notes
Address likely objections proactively.

### Talking Points
- We can preserve continuity while modernizing.
- Pilot metrics will guide broader rollout decisions.
- Governance will be built in from the start.

---

## Slide 11 - Success Metrics

### Slide Title
**How We Will Measure Success**

### On-Slide Content
- developer onboarding time
- deployment frequency
- lead time for changes
- CI/CD cycle time
- security remediation velocity
- AI adoption and usage satisfaction
- retired-tool count / spend reduction

### Speaker Notes
Executives want post-approval accountability.

### Talking Points
- We will measure both efficiency and governance outcomes.
- Success metrics should be reported quarterly.
- Metrics should tie directly to the business case.

---

## Slide 12 - Next Steps

### Slide Title
**Decision and Immediate Next Steps**

### On-Slide Content
1. Approve pilot or platform standardization direction
2. Confirm budget envelope and sponsorship
3. Launch baseline/TCO assessment
4. Stand up pilot teams and success metrics

### Speaker Notes
End with a crisp call to action.

### Talking Points
- The immediate decision is whether to proceed with a structured GitHub standardization motion.
- We can start small, measure rigorously, and scale based on evidence.
- Delay mainly preserves current friction and postpones AI-enabled gains.
